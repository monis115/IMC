Semey State Medical University

Affordable Medical Education in Semey, Kazakhstan

About the University

Founded in 1953, Semey State Medical University (SSMU), formerly Semipalatinsk State Medical Institute, is one of Kazakhstan’s leading medical universities. Located in Semey, it is recognized by WHO, NMC (India), and UNESCO. With over 4,000 students and 450+ faculty, SSMU has trained 25,000+ medical professionals, contributing significantly to global healthcare.

Academic Programs

SSMU offers a 6-year MBBS program in English (5 years + 1-year internship), accredited by WHO and NMC, alongside programs in dentistry, pediatrics, pharmacy, and medico-prophylactic fields. The curriculum emphasizes practical training in affiliated hospitals, with no entrance exam required except NEET for Indian students. Over 3,500 students study medical courses.

Campus & Facilities

SSMU’s campus in Semey, on the Irtysh River, features modern labs, libraries, and its own hospital. Hostels, 200m from campus, offer Wi-Fi, Indian mess, and laundry. Students access clinical training, sports facilities, and cultural events. Semey’s rich history and attractions, like museums, enhance student life despite extreme weather (-45°C to +45°C).

Rankings & Accreditation

SSMU ranks 6th among Kazakhstan’s medical universities and is accredited by WHO, NMC, and UNESCO. Listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools, its MBBS degree is globally recognized, enabling graduates to practice after passing FMGE. Kazakhstan’s medical programs are noted for higher FMGE passing rates.

Student Life

SSMU fosters a vibrant community with 4,000+ students from India, Pakistan, and beyond. Cultural festivals, sports, and academic conferences enrich campus life. Semey’s low cost of living, Indian mess, and historical attractions like the Irtysh River create a welcoming environment. Visa support and a ragging-free campus are provided.
