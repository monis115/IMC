Kokshetau State University

Affordable Medical Education in Kokshetau, Kazakhstan

About the University

Founded in 1962, Kokshetau State University (KSU), named after Shokan Ualikhanov, is a leading public institution in Kokshetau, Kazakhstan. Recognized by WHO, NMC (India), and the Ministry of Education and Science of Kazakhstan, KSU offers quality education in medicine, natural sciences, and teacher education, attracting international students with its affordable programs and modern facilities.

Academic Programs

KSU provides a 6-year MBBS program in English (5 years + 1-year internship), accredited by WHO and NMC, alongside programs in dentistry, nursing, pharmacy, and teacher education. With 65 bachelor’s, 30 master’s, and 6 doctoral programs, the curriculum includes practical training and clinical exposure. Indian students require NEET qualification, with no additional entrance exams.

Campus & Facilities

Located in Kokshetau, a scenic city with lakes and mountains, KSU’s campus features modern infrastructure, including clinical training centers, research labs, and a library. Hostels offer Wi-Fi, Indian mess, and laundry services. The university’s clinical partners provide hands-on experience, while sports facilities and cultural clubs enrich student life.

Rankings & Accreditation

KSU is accredited by WHO, NMC, and listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools. It ranks 33rd in Kazakhstan and 6796th globally (THE 2025), 10th nationally, and 9th in ARES-2020. Its teacher education programs are top 3 nationally, and the MBBS degree is globally recognized, enabling graduates to practice after passing FMGE.

Student Life

KSU fosters a vibrant community with clubs, athletics, and cultural events, supporting a diverse student body. Indian students enjoy familiar cuisine at the Indian mess. Kokshetau’s low cost of living, scenic beauty, and cultural attractions enhance the experience. The university offers visa support, exchange programs, and a ragging-free environment.
